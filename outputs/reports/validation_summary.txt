==========================================================================================
  SCIENTIFIC INTERPRETATION GUIDE
==========================================================================================
  This section provides a guide for interpreting the validation metrics and plots:

  1. MEAN ABSOLUTE PERCENTAGE ERROR (MAPE):
     - MAPE is useful as a relative error metric, but it should not be used alone.
       Specifically, when HYSYS values are close to zero, MAPE can inflate
       disproportionately or become undefined due to division by zero.

  2. MEAN ABSOLUTE ERROR (MAE) & ROOT MEAN SQUARE ERROR (RMSE):
     - MAE and RMSE show error magnitude in the original unit.
     - MAE represents the average linear error, whereas RMSE squashes deviations
       quadratically, making it more sensitive to transient spikes and large lags.

  4. MEAN BIAS ERROR (MBE) & PERCENT BIAS (PBIAS):
     - Bias or PBIAS indicates whether Python systematically overpredicts or underpredicts HYSYS.
       Positive bias shows overprediction; negative bias indicates underprediction.

  5. COEFFICIENT OF DETERMINATION (R2):
     - R2 indicates whether Python follows the variation pattern of HYSYS, but
       it should not be interpreted as agreement by itself. Perfect correlation
       (R2=1) can still occur alongside structural offsets (biases).
